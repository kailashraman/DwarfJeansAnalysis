# SHMR-Weighted SatGen Prior on $(r_s, \rho_s)$

## Setup

The `satgen` family of priors injects ΛCDM-with-tidal-stripping
information into the Stage 2 halo inference by drawing
$(\log_{10} r_s, \log_{10} \rho_s)$ from the Diemer $m12res8\_10k$
subhalo catalog generated by the companion `SatGen_Dwarf` repo. The
`satgen_shmr` variant *additionally* conditions the catalog on a
stellar–halo mass relation (SHMR) and on the observed Galactocentric
distance, producing a **per-dwarf** prior table. The functional form
of the prior is identical to `satgen` (marginal-then-conditional
Gaussian, see [`stage2.md`](stage2.md) § SatGen-conditioned prior);
the difference is in how the lookup table is constructed.

## Step 1: NFW parameters from the catalog

Each halo in the SatGen catalog ships with $(v_{\max}, r_{\max})$. Under
the NFW profile the circular velocity peaks at $x_{\max} = r_{\max}/r_s$
with $x_{\max} \approx 2.16258$ (the root of $d v_c / dr = 0$ for NFW).
Inverting:

$$r_s = \frac{r_{\max}}{x_{\max}} \approx 0.46241\, r_{\max},
\qquad
\rho_s = \frac{x_{\max}^3}{4\pi\, \mu(x_{\max})}\,
         \frac{v_{\max}^2}{G\, r_{\max}^2}
       \approx 1.72126\,\frac{v_{\max}^2}{G\, r_{\max}^2}$$

with $\mu(x) = \ln(1+x) - x/(1+x)$. The numerical constants
(`RS_OVER_RMAX = 0.46241029979236`, `RHOS_COEFF = 1.7212585601570`)
are duplicated verbatim in `scripts/build_satgen_prior_table.py` and
`scripts/build_satgen_shmr_prior_tables.py` so both builders place
the same halo at the same $(r_s, \rho_s)$.

Halos with non-finite or non-positive $(r_s, \rho_s)$ or with
$\log_{10} r_s$ outside `LOG10_RS_BOUNDS` $= [-2, 1]$ are dropped
before any weighting; the surviving mask `in_range` aligns row-for-row
with the upstream weights array.

## Step 2: per-dwarf weights from `SatGen_Dwarf`

The companion repo stores, for each study-sample galaxy, a precomputed
weight array of the form

$$w_i^{(j)} \;=\;
   \underbrace{
     \frac{f_{\rm obs}\!\bigl(\,\hat\ell_i^{(j)}\bigr)}
          {\hat p_{\rm cat}^{(j)}\!\bigl(\,\hat\ell_i^{(j)}\bigr)}
   }_{\text{SHMR importance ratio}}
   \;\cdot\;
   \mathbb{1}\!\left[\,
     \tfrac{1}{2} \le \tfrac{D_{\rm GC}(i)}{D_{\rm GC}^{\text{obs}}} \le 2
   \right]$$

at `data/additional/weights_gc/Diemer/<study_name>.npz` (key
`mstar_weights`), with rows aligned to the full Diemer catalog and
columns indexed by SHMR through `SHMR_INDEX` (10 SHMRs total).
Notation:

- $\hat\ell_i^{(j)}$ is the SHMR-predicted $\log_{10} M_\star$ for
  halo $i$ under SHMR $j$. For SHMR variants with intrinsic scatter
  (e.g. Fattahi+18 / Santos-Santos+22 piecewise scatter), the
  upstream code draws $\hat\ell_i \sim \mathcal{N}(\mathrm{mean}(V_{\max}),\,
  \mathrm{scatter}(V_{\max}))$ rather than returning the deterministic
  mean — so $\hat\ell$ is itself a noisy SHMR realisation, not the
  central relation.
- $f_{\rm obs}(\ell)$ is a **two-sided (split-)normal in
  $\log_{10} M_\star$** centred on the dwarf's observed stellar mass
  with the published asymmetric error bars. The asymmetry comes from
  the *observation*, not from the SHMR's intrinsic scatter.
- $\hat p_{\rm cat}^{(j)}(\ell)$ is a 1-D KDE of the catalog
  distribution of $\hat\ell_i^{(j)}$.

So the SHMR factor is a Bayesian importance ratio that re-weights the
catalog from $\hat p_{\rm cat}^{(j)}$ to $f_{\rm obs}$ — **not** a
plain Gaussian likelihood at the dwarf's observed $\log M_\star$ with
the SHMR's intrinsic scatter. (If you only need the conceptual shape
of the weight to read the rest of this doc, treat it as
"halo $i$ gets re-weighted by how plausible its predicted
$M_\star$ is given the dwarf's observed $M_\star$, divided by how
common that predicted $M_\star$ is in the catalog.")

The geometric gate is a hard binary indicator: halos outside the
factor-of-2 $D_{\rm GC}$ window contribute zero weight. If the gate
would empty the catalog (no halos pass), the upstream `get_mask`
silently widens to $D_{\rm GC}(i) > \tfrac{1}{2}\max_i D_{\rm GC}(i)$
as a fallback. Typical zero-weight fractions in the resulting tables
are large (see `metadata.zero_weight_fraction` in any built table).

Four SHMRs are wired through the production CLIs
(`run_production.py`, `run_batch.py`, `plot_posteriors.py`) by the
`--shmr` flag: `fattahi18`, `moster18`, `danieli23_const`, `kim24`.
The remaining six (`behroozi13`, `moster13`, `rodriguezpuebla17`,
`behroozi19`, `munshi21`, `danieli23_grow`) are available in the
builder but not exposed at the CLI by default.

## Step 3: weighted marginal CDF of $\log_{10} r_s$

After masking to `in_range & (w > 0)`, sort by $x \equiv \log_{10} r_s$
to get $(x_{(1)}, \dots, x_{(N)})$ with companion weights $w_{(i)}$.
The empirical weighted CDF is the cumulative weight fraction,

$$F(x_{(k)}) \;=\; \frac{1}{\Sigma w}\sum_{i \le k} w_{(i)}.$$

The shipped table stores this CDF resampled onto a uniform grid of
$N_{\rm CDF} = 1024$ points across `LOG10_RS_BOUNDS` via `np.interp`,
clamped to $[0, 1]$ at the bounds and monotonised with
`np.maximum.accumulate`.

## Step 4: per-bin conditional $\log_{10} \rho_s \mid \log_{10} r_s$

Bin edges are placed at uniform quantiles of cumulative weight: with
$\tilde c_i \equiv (C_i - \tfrac12 w_{(i)}) / \Sigma w$ the midpoint
cumulative weight at the $i$-th sorted halo, the $(n_{\rm bins}+1)$
bin edges are $\mathtt{np.interp}\bigl(\linspace(0,1,n_{\rm bins}+1),\,
\tilde c,\, x_{(\cdot)}\bigr)$. This equalises **cumulative weight per
bin**, not effective sample size per bin — see [open issue
#1](../open-issues.md) for the consequences when intra-bin weight
distributions are heavy-tailed.

Within bin $k$, with $\{(y_i, w_i)\}$ the conditioned
$\log_{10} \rho_s$ values and their weights:

$$\mu_k = \frac{\sum_i w_i y_i}{\sum_i w_i},
\qquad
\sigma_k^2 = \frac{\sum_i w_i (y_i - \mu_k)^2}
                  {\sum_i w_i - (\sum_i w_i^2)/\sum_i w_i}.$$

The denominator $\Sigma w - \Sigma w^2 / \Sigma w$ is the
reliability-weighted Bessel correction: it reduces to $N - 1$ for
unit weights and to $2 w_1 w_2 / (w_1 + w_2)$ for two-point samples,
guaranteeing $\sigma$ is the unbiased estimator under the assumption
that weights are inverse-variance reliabilities of independent draws
from a common distribution. (Both limits are pinned in
`tests/unit/test_priors_satgen_shmr.py::test_weighted_mean_std_*`.)
The bin centre $\bar x_k$ is the weighted mean of $x$ within the bin,
used as the abscissa when the loader interpolates $\mu, \sigma$
between bins.

## Step 5: adaptive $n_{\rm bins}$

Default $n_{\rm bins} = 300$. The builder reduces it to
$\max(50,\, \lfloor \mathrm{ESS} / 30 \rfloor)$ if the global
effective sample size $\mathrm{ESS} = (\Sigma w)^2 / \Sigma w^2$ does
not provide 30 effective halos per bin on average; below 50 bins the
builder raises rather than emit a degraded table.

This is a **global** floor: it does not protect any individual bin
when the weight distribution is heterogeneous within bins. The
per-bin ESS array is recorded in the metadata
(`ess_per_bin_min`, `ess_per_bin_median`) for downstream diagnostics
and motivates [open issue #1](../open-issues.md).

## Step 6: prior transform

The loader (`_load_satgen_table` in `src/dwarfjeans/jeans/priors.py`,
shared with the un-weighted `satgen` variant) returns
`(grid, cdf, bin_centers, mu, sigma)`. The 4D transform built by
`make_satgen_shmr_prior_transform` maps the unit-cube draw
$u = (u_0, u_1, u_2, u_3)$ to

$$\begin{aligned}
V        &= V_{\rm c} + (2 u_0 - 1) \cdot V_{\rm halfwidth}, \\
\log_{10} r_s &= F^{-1}(u_1)
              \quad \text{via } \mathtt{np.interp}(u_1,\, \text{cdf},\, \text{grid}), \\
\log_{10} \rho_s &= \mu(\log_{10} r_s)
                  + \sigma(\log_{10} r_s) \cdot \Phi^{-1}(u_2)
              \quad \text{with } \mu, \sigma \text{ linearly interpolated between } \bar x_k, \\
\tilde\beta &= \tilde\beta_{\rm lo}
             + (\tilde\beta_{\rm hi} - \tilde\beta_{\rm lo}) \cdot u_3.
\end{aligned}$$

$\Phi^{-1}$ is `scipy.special.ndtri` (the bare inverse-normal CDF;
$\sim$10–50× faster than `scipy.stats.norm.ppf` in this hot loop, and
clipping $u_2 \in [10^{-15},\, 1 - 10^{-15}]$ avoids $\pm\infty$ at
the extremes). The 7D `_with_nuisances` analogue appends the same
$(d, \varepsilon, r_{1/2,{\rm arcmin}})$ block as the loguniform
prior — distance and half-light radius are Gaussian, ellipticity is
truncated-normal on $[0,1]$.

## Build and storage

```
~/.conda/envs/DwarfJeans/bin/python scripts/build_satgen_shmr_prior_tables.py \
    --shmr <SHMR_KEY> [--only key1,key2,...]
```

Per-dwarf output: `data/satgen_prior/<SHMR_KEY>/<lvdb_key>.npz`,
plus a `build_summary.json` per SHMR collecting the metadata blocks
across the study sample. These tables are an explicit exception to
the "don't commit generated outputs" rule (see
`CLAUDE.md` and the corresponding memory): the upstream weights are
themselves derived data and we want the inference fully reproducible
from a fresh checkout without re-running the SatGen_Dwarf pipeline.

## Notes on validity

- **The reliability-Bessel correction assumes the weights are
  inverse-variance reliabilities of independent draws.** The actual
  weights are SHMR importance ratios (Step 2) times an indicator —
  importance weights, not reliabilities. This is the standard mild
  abuse made whenever weighted Gaussian moments are reported on
  importance-resampled catalogs; it is exact only in the equal-weight
  limit. The per-bin sample sizes $n_{\rm per\_bin}$ and effective
  sample sizes $\mathrm{ESS}_k$ are stored in the metadata so
  downstream consumers can quantify how loose the approximation is
  per bin (and see [open issue #1](../open-issues.md)).

- **Per-bin Gaussian is an approximation.** As for the un-weighted
  `satgen` prior, pooled-residual skew and excess kurtosis are small
  (see [`stage2.md`](stage2.md) § SatGen-conditioned prior); the
  per-bin Gaussian approximation is faithful in the bulk for the
  SHMRs tested to date.

- **The geometric gate is a hard prior, not a likelihood.** Halos
  outside the factor-of-2 GC window contribute zero weight and are
  dropped before binning. There is no soft taper; this is conservative
  but means $D_{\rm GC}$ measurement uncertainty does not propagate
  into the prior. Acceptable when $\sigma(D_{\rm GC}) / D_{\rm GC}
  \ll 1$ (true for every galaxy in the study sample).

- **Linear interpolation between bin centres.** The conditional
  Gaussian parameters $\mu(x), \sigma(x)$ are returned by `np.interp`
  between weighted bin centres. The prose Gaussian in Step 4 is a
  *bin-resident* density; the *evaluated* density at a query
  $\log_{10} r_s$ uses linearly-interpolated parameters between
  adjacent bin centres. The two coincide at bin centres and drift
  smoothly between them; the size of the drift is bounded by the
  bin-to-bin variation in $(\mu, \sigma)$, which is small wherever
  per-bin statistics are well-estimated (the same caveat that drives
  [open issue #1](../open-issues.md)).
